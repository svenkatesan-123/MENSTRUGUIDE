How to manage oily hair during menstruation

The oiliness of hair during menstruation is caused by hormonal fluctuations that can stimulate the production of sebum, the natural oil produced by the scalp. Here are some tips to help manage oily hair during menstruation:

1. Wash your hair regularly : Washing your hair regularly can help remove excess oil and prevent it from building up on your scalp. Use a gentle shampoo and avoid over-washing, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more oily.

2. Use a clarifying shampoo : A clarifying shampoo can help remove buildup from hair products and excess oil, leaving your hair feeling clean and fresh. Look for a clarifying shampoo that is gentle and does not contain harsh chemicals.

3. Use dry shampoo : Dry shampoo can help absorb excess oil and extend the time between washes. Apply dry shampoo to your roots and work it into your scalp with your fingers.

4. Avoid touching your hair : Touching your hair can transfer oil from your hands to your hair, making it more oily. Try to avoid touching your hair throughout the day.

5. Use hair accessories : Use hair accessories such as headbands, hair ties, and hats to keep hair away from your face and prevent oil from transferring to your skin.

6. Avoid using heavy styling products : Heavy styling products such as gels and hairsprays can make your hair more oily. Use lightweight styling products that do not contain alcohol or silicone.

By following these tips, you can help manage the oiliness of hair during menstruation and keep your hair looking and feeling healthy. If the oiliness persists or is severe, consult a healthcare professional to rule out any underlying medical conditions.
