How skin changes during the Menstrual Cycle

The skin can change in various ways during the menstrual cycle due to hormonal fluctuations. These changes can include the following:

1. Acne breakouts : Hormonal fluctuations during the menstrual cycle can cause an increase in oil production, leading to breakouts and acne.

2. Dark spots : Some women may experience dark spots or hyperpigmentation during their menstrual cycle. This is due to an increase in melanin production, which is influenced by hormones.

4. Swelling and bloating : Hormonal changes during the menstrual cycle can cause fluid retention, leading to swelling and bloating in the face and other body parts.

5. Rosacea flare-ups : Women with rosacea may experience worsening symptoms during their menstrual cycle. This is believed to be due to hormonal fluctuations that cause increased blood flow to the skin and make it more sensitive.

It's important to remember that these changes can vary from person to person, and some women may not experience any skin changes during their menstrual cycle. If you are concerned about the way your skin is changing, you should consult a dermatologist for personalized advice and treatment options.
