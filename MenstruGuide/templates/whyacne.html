Why acne during Menstruation

Acne breakouts during the menstrual cycle are common and can be attributed to hormonal changes. During the menstrual cycle, hormone levels fluctuate, leading to an increase in oil production. This increase in oil can cause clogged pores, leading to breakouts and acne.

Additionally, the hormone testosterone, which is present in both men and women, can also contribute to acne during the menstrual cycle. Testosterone stimulates the production of sebum, an oily substance that can clog pores and lead to breakouts.

It's also important to note that stress, diet, and skincare habits can also contribute to acne breakouts during the menstrual cycle. To minimize the risk of flights, it's essential to maintain a healthy diet, limit stress, and use gentle skincare products that are non-comedogenic. If you are struggling with persistent acne, it may be helpful to speak with a dermatologist for personalized advice and treatment options.
