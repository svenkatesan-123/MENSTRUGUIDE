Vaginal Itching And Discomfort

The reason of vaginal and vulvar itching will determine the best course of treatment. Home remedies like applying natural yoghurt or honey or soaking in a warm bath with baking soda may be helpful. To get a thorough therapy, it is usually best to visit a doctor.

1. Bath oil

Dry skin can occasionally cause an itchy vulva. The National Eczema Association (NEA) suggests hydrating the skin by incorporating mild oils into bathwater. Any bath oil with aroma should be avoided, as they can aggravate the region further.

2. Baking soda

Adding a quarter cup of baking soda to a bath or using it as a paste on the skin are two common ways to ease itching, according to the NEA. For additional itching relief, the Australian Menopause Society (AMS) advises bathing the vulva with a weak baking soda solution. For every litre of water, they advise putting 1/2 a teaspoon of baking soda.

3. Oatmeal

Oatmeal can also help relieve vulvar itching brought on by dry skin or skin diseases like eczema. A 2015 small study with 29 female participants found that consuming colloidal oatmeal considerably decreased the itch's intensity.

4. Vitamin E

According to research, vitamin E may reduce vulvar or vaginal itching brought on by atopic dermatitis or menopause. For instance, a 2016 study on the benefits of vitamin E suppositories found that they were effective in treating vaginal atrophy in women who had started the menopause (Trusted Source). The vaginal walls' drying, thinning, and inflammation are referred to as "vaginal atrophy". There are several different kinds of vitamin E for vaginal health, including suppositories and lotions. The AMS also states that oral or topically administered vitamin E has this effect.

5. Yogurt and honey

Lactobacillus, a kind of bacteria that also resides in the genitalia, is a component of yoghurt. According to some research, treating fungal infections that may be the cause of vaginal itching by putting yoghurt and honey on the vulva or inside the vagina may be beneficial. According to a tiny 2015 study Trusted Source, people who used a vaginal cream, yoghurt, and honey concoction reported fewer symptoms of a yeast infection. According to a more recent study published in 2021, the anti-yeast remedy clotrimazole may not be superior to honey and yoghurt in terms of effectiveness. However, the authors of a 2019 publication from the Institute for Quality and Efficiency in Health Care, titled "Trusted Source," issued a warning that there is currently insufficient information.

6. Garlic

Some people think that injecting garlic within the vagina or ingesting garlic capsules can treat or prevent bacterial or fungal infections. According to a 2015 assessment by Trusted Source, there isn't enough proof that this treatment is effective, and it could cause burns and allergic responses among other side effects. Yet, a case study from 2018Trusted Source did shown that a subject's vaginal infection vanished as a result of the use of garlic in this way.
