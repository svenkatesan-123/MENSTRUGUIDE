How hairs change during menstruation

Many women experience changes in their hair during their menstrual cycle due to fluctuations in hormones. Here are some ways your hair may be affected during your period:

1. Shedding : During the menstrual cycle, there is an increase in the hormone progesterone, which can cause hair to enter the resting phase of the growth cycle. This can lead to increased shedding of hair during the menstrual period.

2. Dryness : Hormonal changes can also lead to dryness and lack of moisture in the hair. This can cause hair to become brittle, frizzy, and prone to breakage.

3. Oiliness : Hormonal changes can also lead to increased oil production in the scalp, which can cause hair to become greasy and oily.

4. Dullness : Hormonal changes can cause a decrease in blood flow to the scalp, leading to a lack of nutrients and a dull, lackluster appearance of hair.

To help manage these hair changes during your period, here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are free of harsh chemicals and fragrances.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to support overall hair health.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment to help moisturize and nourish hair.
  • Consider using hair products designed for oily or dry hair depending on your individual needs.
  • Talk to your healthcare provider if you are experiencing severe hair changes or hair loss during your menstrual cycle. They may recommend additional treatments or tests to help manage your symptoms.
