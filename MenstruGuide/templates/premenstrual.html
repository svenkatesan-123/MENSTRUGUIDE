PREMENSTRUAL SYNDROME

Many women have premenstrual syndrome (PMS), a common ailment, before to their period. While there are many medication options for treating PMS, there are also some natural methods that could help with symptoms. Here are a few natural treatments for PMS:

1. Exercise

Engaging in regular exercise will help to lessen PMS symptoms like cramps, mood swings, and exhaustion. Endorphins are also released during exercise, and they help elevate mood and lessen discomfort.

2. Diet

Maintaining a balanced diet can help reduce the symptoms of PMS. Cramping, bloating, and mood swings can be lessened by consuming foods high in calcium, magnesium, and vitamin B6. Leafy greens, dairy products, nuts, seeds, and whole grains are among the foods you should eat. Avoid foods high in salt, coffee, and alcohol, which can make bloating and mood swings worse.

3. Herbal remedies

Chamomile tea, ginger tea, and evening primrose oil are a few herbal treatments that have been proved to help with PMS symptoms.

4. Heat therapy

Putting heat on the lower abdomen helps ease discomfort and cramping. Consider using a hot water bottle or heating pad.

5. Relaxation methods

PMS symptoms can get worse under stress. Deep breathing, yoga, and meditation are relaxation practises that can help lower stress and elevate mood.

6. Sleep

Obtaining enough sleep helps with PMS symptoms reduction and is crucial for general wellness. Strive for 7-8 hours of sleep per night, minimum.

Always seek medical advice if you have severe PMS symptoms or if they are interfering with your everyday life. Keep in mind that these home remedies may not be effective for everyone.
