IRREGULAR PERIODS

Factors that Cause Uneven Periods

  • Anaemia i.e. drop in the blood's overall level of red blood cells (RBCs) or haemoglobin.
  • Weight loss or gain
  • Menopause
  • Thyroid disorders
  • Hormonal imbalance
  • Liver disease
  • Tuberculosis
  • Miscarriage and other health conditions
  • excessive caffeine and alcohol consumption

    • This issue can also arise due to lifestyle factors such as increased smoking, drinking too much alcohol, coffee, travelling, stress, and the use of certain birth control pills and drugs. You can experiment with a variety of home remedies to treat irregular menstruation.

    1. Pineapple

    An enzyme called bromelain is found in pineapples, and it may help to relieve inflammation. Inflammation plays a role in some menstrual cycle irregularities. Although studies have not discovered a connection between the two, some people think that eating pineapple may help control periods. More research is required to determine whether pineapple can help with irregular periods. Like other fruit juices, pineapple juice contains a lot of sugar and is not recommended for diabetics.

    2. Turmeric

    Uneven periods are one health ailment for which turmeric is a well-liked home treatment. Its capacity to control periods, however, is untested. Scientists have discovered that turmeric has a major anti-inflammatory effect. An active component of turmeric called curcumin has been shown in studies to have anti-inflammatory properties.

    3. Apple cider vinegar

    According to preliminary study, consuming apple cider vinegar may help PCOS sufferers control their ovulation. To demonstrate this benefit, however, researchers must conduct additional studies. With only seven participants, the study in question was too tiny to draw any firm conclusions.

    4. Exercise regularly

    Exercise can support regular menstrual periods by regulating hormones, lowering stress, and maintaining a healthy weight.

    5. Reduce stress

    Stress can mess with your hormones and make your periods unpredictable. Stress reduction can be aided by relaxation methods like yoga, meditation, or deep breathing.

    6. Eating healthy diet

    A diet high in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein helps encourage regular menstrual periods by regulating hormones.

    7. Ginger tea

    Ginger has been used for centuries to regulate menstrual cycles. Drinking ginger tea can help to balance hormones and reduce menstrual cramps.

    8. Chasteberry

    Chasteberry, also known as vitex, is an herb that can help regulate hormonal imbalances and promote regular menstrual cycles.

    9. Magnesium-rich foods

    Magnesium is an important mineral for regulating hormones and maintaining menstrual health. Foods high in magnesium include spinach, almonds, and avocados.

    10. Vitamin B6

    Vitamin B6 is an important nutrient for regulating hormones. Foods high in vitamin B6 include chickpeas, salmon, and bananas.

    It's important to note that these remedies are not guaranteed to work for everyone and it's always best to consult with a doctor before trying any new remedies.
